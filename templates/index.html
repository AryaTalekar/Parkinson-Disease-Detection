Frequently Asked Questions

What are the early signs of Parkinson‚Äôs disease?

The earliest symptoms include: 1.Tremors (shaking hands, fingers, or chin) 2.Slower movements (bradykinesia) 3.Stiffness in muscles 4.Balance and posture issues 5.Reduced facial expressions

What causes Parkinson‚Äôs disease?

The exact cause is unknown, but it's linked to: 1.Loss of dopamine-producing brain cells 2.Genetic factors (family history increases risk) 3.Environmental triggers (toxins, pesticides, heavy metals)

Is Parkinson‚Äôs disease curable?

There is no cure, but treatments help manage symptoms. These include: 1.Medications (Levodopa, dopamine agonists) 2.Physical therapy and exercise 3.Surgery (Deep Brain Stimulation for severe cases)

Can Parkinson‚Äôs disease be prevented?

There is no definite way to prevent it, but a healthy lifestyle, avoiding toxins, and regular exercise may reduce risk.

What are the stages of Parkinson‚Äôs disease?

Parkinson‚Äôs progresses in five stages: 1.Mild symptoms (tremors, subtle movement changes) 2.Increased stiffness and tremors (daily activities slightly affected) 3.Balance issues, slow movements (fall risk increases) 4.Severe disability (needs assistance with daily tasks) 5.Advanced stage (wheelchair-bound, requires full-time care)
